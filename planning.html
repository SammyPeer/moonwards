The Virtual Moon Colony Project: Cernan's Promise VMC

Currently working on: First Draft

We're building the first draft now. It develops how to build an extensive permanent colony on the Moon. For this initial set-up, we'll go with the parameters as set out in the table. Things can be added or adjusted as more information and talent come in. The main thing is that this is a vision of what could be done if there was a serious drive for settlement, where a group of nations considered it important enough to devote significant funds to the task. Cernan's Promise is the seed of much, much greater things. Its goals are permanence, scale, flexibility, and ecosystem creation.

This is a collaborative project open to anyone who can pitch in. If you are interested in participating, leave a message in the forum or email me, kim@moonwards.com. What should be added here? What needs improving? What would you like to work on? What do you think?

Installations and Equipment: To maximize the utility of a limited amount of machinery and supplies, these are the design principles:

  • Modular - Everything that can be shared around, will be. Design things that can be dropped quickly and easily into a number of machines for a quick repair, added capacity, or specialized ability.
  • Light - Use not only materials with the best density and strength for the lightest design, but also always look for ways to use local materials - rocks and soil for stability, insulation, or thermal mass, for instance. Remember that many pieces will arrive on the 2nd mission and there could be opportunities to have very simple structural elements be provided by materials made by the 1st mission.
  • Simple - Low part count, simplest possible technology. Electric or mechanical actuators instead of hydraulic, for example. Design parts that can be replaced with parts fabricated on site where possible - 3d printed with sintered metal powder or made of fused basalt.
  • Feasible - Everything needs to be designed and developed, but don't use a technology unless there is high confidence it would be available to a real colony. It is reasonable to assume more agile and powerful robotics, for instance, but don't bet on fusion power.

Structures

Structures: This is a permanent colony. Build for growth and for the ages:

  • Low Radiation - Above-ground portions of buildings need 4.5 m of hard-packed regolith or rock to reduce exposure to cosmic radiation to a safe level for someone living there for decades. In the near term, structures with an above-ground wall thickness of 1.5 m is sufficient. Padding such areas with another 3 m of packed regolith would be done during the first year or two of human occupancy, in that time span the higher radiation dose is acceptable.
  • Contain Full Atmosphere - Though it may prove better to employ a thinner atmosphere with a higher oxygen content at times, assume that all structures will eventually contain a full atmosphere. That is a lot of outward pressure on surfaces above ground. Even walls 4.5 m thick might not be enough to contain it if they are made mostly of packed regolith. Use of arches that can be placed under tension with cables is desirable, consider materials and spans carefully elsewhere.
  • Lots of Sun - People will live here long-term. Make it a pleasant place to be. Living in artificial lighting is depressing. There should be enough sunlight for most plants to grow, simply for comfort, as well as to have plants. Big windows are a big problem, so bring in lots of sun with parabolic mirrors through small windows, remembering to ensure there is no line-of-sight straight to the outdoors falling where people will pass their time, exposing them to radiation. Consider carefully how to make best use of that sun with solar turbines, which leads to the next point.
  • Thermal Mass - Noon is really hot, midnight is really, really cold. But the sun is the same as what falls here on Earth, just not redistributed by an atmosphere and oceans. If you give it enough mass to soak into, temperatures will stay within a comfortable range with little to no heat control equipment. Enough sun to run a solar turbine and grow plants will require lots of such mass. Get as much rock into buildings as possible, either in the foundations and walls, or simply placed inside. If you can thermally bridge to a nearby massive boulder, do so - better yet, find a way to bridge to the mega-regolith layer.
  • Build Big - As big as possible. With the above requirements satisfied, the task is to figure out how to build a structure with the largest possible volume with available resources.
  • Create Views - Though it comes last, this makes the list. Being in a place without windows is crazy-making. Not only that, remember the first buildings in particular need to maintain inspiration for the people back home. Windows must exist, but must be extremely well designed in order to give a sense of openness without sacrificing safety. Internal spaces that feel like the outdoors are also critical.

Environment

The nature of the Moon environment and the specifics of the area and region where the colony will go have to be researched, and then built as a digital world. Their are many aspects to this, and the better each can be described and modelled, the more relevant the virtual colony is. As data comes in adjustments will need to be made, which may then show that adjustments need to be made to colony designs.

Mission Planning

Understanding what equipment is needed, when, depends on having a plan. This part is critical to building this into an activity that people find engaging, something that feels sort of like a hobby. I have resisted using the word 'game' because that makes me think of winning and losing, time limits, and just the wrong sort of pacing and energy. Minecraft and Sims are games that might be comparable, but those games still have tasks that need to be done or you 'lose'. I think of this more like making a model, or Lego. (Maybe i should approach Lego, actually...) When there is enough done to make this an activity where people can play at building the colony, that needs to feel real.

Platforms

How are people going to use our creations? How will they be part of our work? My sense is we have at least two main audiences: the people who enjoy building Moonwards (the project), and the people who enjoy exploring and playing at building in Cernan's Promise (the product). This page is mostly for people who want to build Moonwards - people who want to make the show. The start page of the site, where the current project is on display, is for the final, broader audience who want to enjoy the show. This page's audience requires a lot more commitment and knowledge, but both audiences need to have fun and feel they are getting something out of the experience. So, this is about 'UX', user experience. Also 'PR', public relations. I'm going to put them together and call them PRUX, because nobody can stop me.
